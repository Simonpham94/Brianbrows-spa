Eyebrow & Eyeliner

  • DO NOT work out the day before the procedure.
  • DO NOT tan or have sunburns on your face, or you will be required to reschedule.
  • DO NOT take aspirin, niacin, vitamin E, fish oil, or ibuprofen 24 hours before the procedure.
  • No steroid shots 30 days before the procedure
  • DO NOT have alcohol or caffeine one day before and after the procedure, as this helps minimize any oozing or swelling after the procedure.
  • No tweezing or waxing at least 48 hours before the procedure; electrolysis no less than 5 days before.
  • Eyelash or eyebrow tinting should be done at least 48 hours before the procedure.
  • DO NOT use any lash growing serums at least 4 weeks before the procedure, ortherwise the procedure cannot be performed
  • DO NOT use botox 4 weeks before your procedure.
  • Please note that you will be more sensitive during your menstrual cycle.
  • For Microblading: 4 hours following the procedure, clean your eyebrows with sterile water and cotton, 2 times per day in the next 5 days. Let it dry naturally, and keep them dry for 10 days.
  • For Powdery: On your first day, please clean them every half hour with sterile water until you go to bed. Afterwards, 2 times per day in 5 following days. Keep them dry for 10 days.
  • For Eyeliner: Keep your eyes dry for 10 days, you may only clean the area AROUND your eyes (not the tattoo line) with sterile water and Q-tips.
  • NO water, cleansers, creams, or any other products on the treated area for 10 days.
  • DO NOT resume any method of hair removal for at least 2 weeks after the procedure.
  • Eyelash or eyebrow tinting should only be done 2 weeks after the procedure
  • DO NOT use lash growing serums at least 8 weeks after your eyeliner procedure
  • DO NOT rub, pick, or scratch the treated areas. Let any scabbing or dry skin naturally fall off. Picking can cause scarring.
  • Avoid direct sun exposure or tanning 2 weeks after the procedure.
  • Avoid heavy sweating for the first 10 days following your procedure to avoid infection
  • NO chemical treatments, botox, microdermabrasion, and facials 4 weeks after the procedure.
  • Avoid sleeping on your face for the first 10 days.

Lips Permanent Makeup

  • If you desire a lip procedure, it is crucial that you consult with us regarding cold sores, shingles, and Herpes simplex. If you have EVER had any of the listed conditions, it is essential that you are pre-treated with antiviral. This prescription can be provided by your physician
  • For your safety, we recommend you to take antiviral (Valtrex or Zovirax), 1 day in advance, and avoid an episode of herpes or cold sore (please consult with your doctor).
  • Keep your lips moist with vitamin E oil for 2 weeks after the procedure.
  • DO NOT let the area dry out completely; this may cause scabbing and cracking on the skin surface.
  • DO NOT expose tattooed areas to the sun or tanning beds without using sunblock. This may cause premature fading.
  • DO NOT eat or drink citrus (fruit and juice), greasy, salty, oily or spicy food until your lips are completely healed.
  • While eating, DO NOT constantly wipe your lips with napkins or licking your lips as this will impede the healing process.
  • Be careful when brushing your teeth. Toothpaste may pull the pigment out of the lip vermilion.
  • DO NOT bleach your teeth while your lips are healing.

Tatto Removal

  • Keep the area clean and open to the air, DO NOT cover it.
  • It is important that you keep the treated area dry until it's fully scabbed
  • Avoid bathing, swimming, saunas, hot tubs and intense exercises after the procedure.
  • DO NOT apply lotions, ointments, makeups on the treated area until it is completely healed and scabs fall off naturally. This may take at least 2 weeks.
  • DO NOT pick at the scabs since it may result in scarring.
  • Once all the scabs fall off naturally, apply one drop of vitamin E oil on the treated area 4-6 times throughout the day, every other day, for at least 4 weeks, or until your next session. DO NOT start applying vitamin E oil until all the scabs fall off and keep the area as dry as possible during scabbing.
  • For lips tattoo removal, it is advised that you drink all liquids with a straw, and cut your food in small pieces until your scabs fall off naturally.
